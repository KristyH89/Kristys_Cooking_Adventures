ALGORITHM Oven-Baked Wild Boar Ribs with Braised Red Cabbage and Mashed Potatoes

WILD BOAR RIBS

INGREDIENTS

1 rack wild boar ribs (or pork ribs)

DRY RUB

5 tbsp brown sugar

1 tbsp coarse sea salt

2 tsp mild paprika powder

2 tsp garlic powder

2 tsp onion powder

GLAZE

3 cloves garlic, finely chopped

1 onion, finely chopped

75 ml soy sauce

100 ml ketjap manis

5 tbsp honey

1 tbsp olive oil

PROCESS

Mix all ingredients for the dry rub.

Remove the membrane from the back of the ribs if present.

Rub the ribs generously with the spice mixture.

Let marinate for at least 1 hour, preferably overnight.

Preheat the oven to 110–115°C.

Place the ribs on a baking tray and cook for approximately 3 hours.

Wrap the ribs tightly in aluminium foil with a splash of apple juice, cola, or water.

Return to the oven and cook for another 2 hours.

Meanwhile, prepare the glaze.

Sauté the garlic and onion briefly in olive oil.

Mix with the soy sauce, ketjap manis, and honey.

Unwrap the ribs and brush generously with the glaze.

Return to the oven for about 1 hour, brushing several times until sticky and caramelized.


BRAISED RED CABBAGE

INGREDIENTS

1 small red cabbage (approximately 800 g), finely sliced

50 g butter

1 onion, sliced

2 tbsp apple cider vinegar

30 g sugar

2 cinnamon sticks

3 cloves

1 bay leaf

100 ml apple juice

2 apples, peeled and diced

Salt and black pepper to taste

PROCESS

Melt the butter in a large pot.

Cook the onion until soft and translucent.

Add the red cabbage and stir well.

Add vinegar, sugar, cinnamon sticks, cloves, bay leaf, salt, and pepper.

Pour in the apple juice and bring to a gentle simmer.

Cover and cook for 45–60 minutes.

Stir occasionally and add extra apple juice if needed.

Add the diced apples during the final 10–15 minutes of cooking.

Remove the cinnamon sticks, cloves, and bay leaf before serving.


MASHED POTATOES

INGREDIENTS

1.5–2 kg potatoes, peeled

Butter to taste

Warm milk

Salt to taste

ALTERNATIVE

Reserved potato cooking water instead of milk

PROCESS

Peel and cut the potatoes into evenly sized pieces.

Cook in salted water until tender.

Warm the milk gently.

Drain the potatoes.

Add butter and milk.

Mash until smooth and creamy.

Season with salt.

For a dairy-free variation, use some of the reserved cooking water instead of milk.


SERVING SUGGESTION

Serve the wild boar ribs alongside the braised red cabbage and mashed potatoes.

Spoon a little extra glaze over the ribs before serving.


OUTPUT

A hearty countryside meal featuring tender oven-baked wild boar ribs glazed with honey, soy sauce, and ketjap manis, served with sweet braised red cabbage with apples and creamy homemade mashed potatoes.
