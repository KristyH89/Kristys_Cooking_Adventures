ALGORITHM Traditional Egg-Yolk Carbonara (Steam Emulsion Method)

INGREDIENTS

400 g spaghetti

150–200 g pancetta or guanciale, diced

5 egg yolks

80 g Pecorino Romano cheese (or Parmesan), finely grated

Freshly ground black pepper

Salt (for pasta water)

OPTIONAL ADD-ON
1 handful chanterelle mushrooms, sliced

PROCESS

Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil and cook the pasta until al dente.

In a pan, cook the pancetta or guanciale until crispy and the fat has fully rendered.

Drain most of the fat, then reserve a small amount.

In a bowl, combine the egg yolks, grated cheese, black pepper, and a spoonful of the warm pancetta fat.

Mix until it forms a thick, smooth paste.

When de pasta is ready, reserve some hot pasta water in a cup, then drain the rest in another pot.

Place a heatproof bowl over the pot with the hot water (creating a steam bath / bain-marie).

Put the egg mixture into the bowl and gently stir over the steam until it becomes warm, slightly thickened, and creamy.

Gradually add a few spoonfuls of hot pasta water while stirring to loosen and emulsify the mixture.

Remove the bowl from the heat.

Add the drained pasta into the mixture and stir continuously until the sauce becomes silky and coats the pasta.

(Optional step) Add sautéed chanterelle mushrooms, cooked separately in the pancetta pan until golden.

Finally, mix in the crispy pancetta pieces.

Serve immediately with extra grated cheese and freshly ground black pepper.

OUTPUT

A silky, emulsified traditional carbonara made with egg yolks, cheese, and pancetta fat, gently thickened over steam for a rich and creamy texture without scrambling the eggs.
