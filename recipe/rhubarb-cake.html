ALGORITHM Greek Yogurt Cake with Rhubarb (or Apple Variation)

INGREDIENTS

250 g Greek yogurt

75 g olive oil

2 eggs

Zest of 1 lemon

1 tsp vanilla extract

250 g white sugar

225 g flour

2 tsp baking powder

Pinch of salt

300 g rhubarb (or apples as an alternative)

1 tbsp cane sugar

OPTIONAL APPLE VARIATION
1–2 apples, peeled and sliced (can be used instead of or combined with rhubarb)

PROCESS

Preheat the oven to 175°C and line a loaf pan with baking paper (or grease it with butter or baking spray).

In a large bowl, mix Greek yogurt, olive oil, eggs, lemon zest, vanilla extract, and sugar. Beat until smooth and creamy.

In a separate bowl, combine flour, baking powder, and salt.

Add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture and stir until fully combined.

Prepare the rhubarb (or apples) by cutting into evenly sized pieces or slices.

Layer the batter and fruit in the loaf pan: start with one-third of the batter, then one-third of the fruit. Repeat this layering two more times, finishing with fruit on top.

Sprinkle the top layer evenly with cane sugar.

Bake for 55–60 minutes, or until a skewer inserted in the center comes out clean.

Remove from the oven and let cool in the pan for a short while before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely.

Dust with powdered sugar before serving.

OUTPUT

A moist and soft Greek yogurt cake with layers of tangy rhubarb or sweet apple, lightly caramelized on top and perfect for serving with coffee or tea.

TIP

This cake can easily be adapted throughout the seasons. Use rhubarb in spring, berries in summer, or apple and pear with cinnamon in autumn for a warm variation.
